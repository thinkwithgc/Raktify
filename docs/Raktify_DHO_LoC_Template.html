1 Preamble

Whereas the District Health Officer, {District Name} District (hereafter "the DHO Office") is the nodal authority for implementation of the National Blood Policy 2002, the National Hemovigilance Programme, and related public-health programmes in the district;

And whereas the Choudhari EduHealth India Foundation (hereafter "the Foundation") has developed and operates Raktify, a free voluntary blood-donation coordination platform aligned with the Drugs & Cosmetics Act, 1940 and the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023;

And whereas both parties share an interest in strengthening voluntary blood donation, reducing emergency-response time, improving transfusion-transmissible-infection (TTI) traceability, and modernising hemovigilance reporting in {District Name} District;

The parties hereby document the following understanding for cooperation, with no financial obligation on either party.

2 Responsibilities of the Foundation

  • Provide access to the Raktify platform, at raktify.choudhari.ngo, free of charge to all donors, hospitals, blood banks, NGOs and camp organisers in {District Name} District in perpetuity.
  • Provide a read-only district governance dashboard, with secure login, to the DHO Office and any officer the DHO Office nominates.
  • Provide onboarding support to every licensed blood bank and Government hospital in the district within 30 days of their request.
  • Train counter staff at each onboarded blood bank in TTI screening, four-eyes verification, and inventory recording on the platform.
  • Deploy and maintain a district coordinator team to assist with emergency-request triage and donor activation.
  • Maintain all donor and patient personal data in encrypted form at rest, with row-level access controls, an immutable audit log, and data residency within India (Microsoft Azure, Central India region).
  • Submit a quarterly platform-performance summary to the DHO Office, including donations, requests handled, average response time, and any policy-relevant incidents.

3 Responsibilities of the DHO Office

  • Issue an official circular to all licensed blood banks and Government hospitals in {District Name} District directing them to register on the Raktify platform within 30 days of the circular date.
  • Include Raktify onboarding and active usage in the routine quality-audit checklist used during scheduled inspections of blood banks.
  • Designate a nodal officer (typically the Programme Officer, Blood Cell) as the single point of contact for the Foundation's coordinator team.
  • Facilitate introductions between the Foundation and Government hospitals, Civil Surgeon, Medical Superintendents, and the Maharashtra State Blood Transfusion Council as relevant.
  • Provide feedback on platform functionality, with particular reference to the operational requirements of the district's hospitals and blood banks.

4 Data principles and privacy

The parties recognise that compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 is paramount. The following data-handling principles shall be observed at all times:

The Foundation shall not share district donor or patient data with any other district, any other State, or any third party (commercial, research or otherwise) without the prior written consent of the DHO Office, except where such disclosure is required by an order of a court of competent jurisdiction or by a competent regulatory authority.

5 Term, renewal and termination

  • This Letter of Cooperation is initially valid for twelve (12) months from the effective date noted on page 1.
  • Renewal at the end of the term is automatic, by exchange of a brief letter from either party affirming continued cooperation; no fresh signature is required if both parties are satisfied.
  • Either party may withdraw cooperation with thirty (30) days written notice to the other party, with no penalty.
  • On withdrawal, the Foundation shall continue to maintain district data already collected for the statutory retention periods (Drugs & Cosmetics Act: 5 years; hemovigilance: 7 years; audit log: permanent) and shall make it available to the DHO Office upon request during those periods.

6 Nature of this agreement

This is a non-binding Letter of Cooperation, not a commercial contract. No fees are paid by either party to the other. No exclusivity is granted to either party. Neither party assumes any liability for the operational performance of the other. Disputes, if any, shall be resolved by good-faith discussion between the parties; should that fail, the parties shall approach the State Blood Transfusion Council for mediation prior to any other recourse.
