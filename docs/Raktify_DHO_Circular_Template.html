Subject: Mandatory registration on the Raktify voluntary-blood-donation coordination platform by all licensed blood banks and Government hospitals in {District Name} District.

Reference: (i) National Blood Policy 2002 · (ii) Drugs & Cosmetics Act, 1940 and the Rules thereunder · (iii) National Hemovigilance Programme guidelines, National Institute of Biologicals · (iv) Maharashtra State Blood Transfusion Council circulars on voluntary donation strengthening.

In the interest of strengthening voluntary blood donation, reducing emergency-response time for critical-tier blood requests, and improving transfusion-transmissible-infection (TTI) traceability in this district, the undersigned hereby directs that:

  1. All blood banks holding a valid CDSCO licence under the Drugs & Cosmetics Act, 1940 (whether Government, charitable, or in-house hospital-based) operating in {District Name} District shall complete onboarding on the Raktify platform (raktify.choudhari.ngo) within 30 (thirty) days from the date of this circular.
  2. All Government hospitals, civil hospitals, sub-district hospitals, rural hospitals and primary health centres in {District Name} District that raise blood requests shall complete onboarding on the same platform within the same window.
  3. Onboarded institutions shall use the platform for: (a) recording donations and TTI screening; (b) inventory tracking; (c) raising and fulfilling emergency blood requests; (d) recording crossmatch confirmations; (e) submitting monthly hemovigilance data via the platform's automated report export. Use of the platform is free of cost; no fee shall be levied on patients, donors, or institutions.
  4. The Raktify platform is operated by Choudhari EduHealth India Foundation (NGO-DARPAN registration MH/2025/0643345, 80G eligible), a non-profit organisation, under a Letter of Cooperation with this office dated {dd/mm/yyyy}. The Foundation shall provide free onboarding support, volunteer training, and technical assistance to all institutions in {District Name} District.
  5. Compliance with this circular shall be incorporated into the routine quality-audit checklist of this office. The next scheduled inspection of each licensed blood bank shall include verification of Raktify onboarding and active usage. Non-compliance shall be recorded in the inspection report and may be considered during licence renewal review.
  6. Donor and patient privacy under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 shall be maintained at all times. The platform is designed to mask donor mobile numbers from hospital users; institutions shall not attempt to circumvent these privacy safeguards.
  7. A monthly district-level usage report shall be generated by this office through the DHO governance dashboard provided by the platform, and shared with the State Blood Transfusion Council for inclusion in the State hemovigilance return.
  8. The Programme Officer (Blood Cell), this office, is hereby designated as the district nodal officer for Raktify implementation. All implementation queries shall be addressed to the nodal officer in the first instance, and through the nodal officer to the Foundation's coordinator team.

All concerned officers and institutions are directed to take immediate action and report compliance to this office within the stipulated period.
