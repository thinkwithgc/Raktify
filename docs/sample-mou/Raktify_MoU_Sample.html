  2. {{institution_legal_name}}, a {{institution_type}} (license / registration number {{license_number}}), having its address at {{institution_address}} (the “Institution”).

2. Purpose

The Foundation operates Raktify, a community blood donation and emergency matching platform. The Institution wishes to participate in the platform to (a) report and manage its blood inventory, (b) raise blood requests on behalf of its patients, (c) coordinate with voluntary donors mediated by Foundation-trained coordinators, and (d) submit hemovigilance data to the District Health Officer through the platform.

3. Term and Termination

This MoU is effective from {{signing_date}} and remains in force until {{effective_until_date}}, unless terminated earlier by either party with 30 days’ written notice. The Foundation may suspend the Institution’s access immediately upon written notice in the event of suspected misuse, regulatory non-compliance, or material breach of clauses 4 or 5 below.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

4.1 Foundation will

  • Provide the Institution with secure platform access, training material, and a designated coordinator.
  • Maintain platform availability with reasonable industry-standard uptime targets and notify the Institution of planned downtime where possible.
  • Implement and maintain technical and organisational safeguards for patient and donor data as set out in clause 5.
  • Provide hemovigilance reports in formats accepted by the District Health Officer.
  • Not charge the Institution any fees for use of the platform during the term of this MoU.

4.2 Institution will

  • Designate one or more trained staff members as platform users and maintain credential confidentiality.
  • Record blood inventory, donations, and TTI screening data accurately and promptly on the platform.
  • Comply with the Drugs and Cosmetics Act 1940, the Drugs and Cosmetics Rules 1945, and any directions of the CDSCO and the State Drugs Controller relating to blood banks (where applicable).
  • Not share platform-mediated donor contact details with any third party.
  • Report any data breach affecting platform-held data to the Foundation within 24 hours of discovery.

5. Data Protection and Confidentiality

Each party will treat personal data of donors, patients, and platform users as confidential and process it only for the purposes set out in this MoU and in accordance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 and any rules and notifications issued thereunder. The Foundation acts as the Data Fiduciary in respect of donor and patient data captured through the Raktify platform. The Institution acts as a Data Processor in respect of any such data accessed in the course of its operational use of the platform.

Neither party may disclose any data, system credentials, audit logs, or platform-specific commercial information to any third party without the prior written consent of the other party, except as required by law or by a competent regulatory authority.

6. Limitation of Liability

The Foundation provides the Raktify platform on an as-available basis. Clinical decisions, blood-bank operational decisions, and decisions to accept or reject donors based on platform-surfaced data remain the sole responsibility of the Institution and its medical staff. The Foundation is not liable for any direct or indirect loss arising from (a) clinical judgments made by the Institution, (b) failure of any third-party service on which the platform depends, or (c) circumstances beyond the Foundation’s reasonable control including network outages, regulatory directions, or force majeure events.

7. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

This MoU is governed by the laws of India. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this MoU which cannot be resolved through good-faith discussion will be referred to arbitration before a sole arbitrator under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996. The seat of arbitration will be Amravati, Maharashtra. The language of arbitration will be English.
